Ronald Rommel N. Viloria

Ronald Rommel N. Viloria is an entrepreneur, accomplished industrial designer, and the visionary leader behind Viloria Product Design Enterprise. For decades, he has honed his expertise in crafting captivating, market-defining brands for global giants such as Target Corporation, Pottery Barn, Crate & Barrel, and West Elm.

Driven by a philosophy of "designing with intention and purpose; innovating through empathy and collaboration," Mr. Viloria is a dedicated advocate for Filipino craftsmanship and inclusivity.

About

The Designer
Behind the Vision

"I am a consumer-oriented Industrial Designer — every design should satisfy the needs of the consumer."

As Head of Design at Viloria Product Design Enterprise (VPDE Studio) and Design Director at CASA Viloria, he leads extensive product development in furniture, home furnishing, home storage, lighting, graphic design, seasonal craft, visual merchandising, and packaging and exhibition design for both local and international markets.

Known affectionately as the "Big Brother Entrepreneur," Mr. Viloria is a respected thought leader, sought-after speaker, and the founder of Casa Viloria Hub and Casa Viloria Design and Build. He serves as a certified mentor for Go Negosyo and KALYE NEGOSYO, and is an active member of the Philippine Chamber of Commerce and Industry (Antipolo).

He was also the former lead design consultant for the Design Center of the Philippines, where he played a pivotal role in mentoring artisans through the OTOP program. Today, he continues to champion local industry as a brand and innovation consultant for various DTI regions and private enterprises.

He has attended and participated in international shows in France, Italy, Germany, United States, China, Hong Kong, Singapore, Thailand, Indonesia, and Vietnam.

Sustainability Advocacy

Design as a
responsibility

Mr. Viloria is a vocal champion of sustainable design and the circular economy. He challenges businesses to rethink their packaging from the ground up — moving away from the wasteful linear "take, make, dispose" model toward solutions that are reusable, meaningful, and brand-defining.

"Your packaging reflects your values. If your plastic waste ends up in the ocean, your brand ends up there with it."

Advocates for the circular economy — designing packaging that goes back, not into landfill.

Demonstrates how sustainable packaging becomes a marketing asset — creating deeper customer engagement.

Works with La Union clients on repurposed packaging experiences that build lasting brand loyalty.

Supports community-based manufacturing in Payatas — proof that good design and social good coexist.

Design Process

How great design
gets made

01

Discover & Research

Deep consumer research, market analysis, and community immersion. Understanding not just what people want, but what they truly need.

02

Ideate & Sketch

Rapid ideation across multiple directions. Sketching, concept modelling, and material exploration until the right idea surfaces.

03

Develop & Prototype

Refining the chosen concept through detailed design development, material specification, and physical or digital prototyping.

04

Deliver & Sustain

Production-ready design with full documentation, supplier coordination, and ongoing support to ensure the product reaches its audience as intended.
